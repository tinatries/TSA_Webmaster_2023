Apollo 17: Last NASA Human Moon Mission

As we work to move forward towards a more futuristic tomorrow in space travel it's important to acknowledge the missions that have gotten us to this point. Apollo 17 is a prime example of this, as it was NASA’s last mission to the moon due to funding issues. s. It was not without fail though as the bulk of the mission went without fail, and included moments such as the longest moonwalk, clocking in at 7 hours—one of only 3 moonwalks in history

The mission itself also completed the most orbits around the moon at 75 and had geologist Harrison Schmitt walk on the moon's surface. Harrison Schmitt later went on to become a US senator for New Mexico, running a campaign focused on looking toward the future as he had done when he was an astronaut. The other astronaut on Apollo 17’s moonwalk, Gene Cernan, was said to have written his daughter's initials on the moon's surface.
